      Saia, Inc. Garners 2006 Quest for Quality Award

      DULUTH, GA - September 18, 2006 - Leading less-than-truckload (LTL) carrier Saia, Inc. (NASDAQ:SAIA) has been recognized by Logistics Management magazine as a 2006 Quest For Quality award winner.

      The 23-year-old Quest For Quality survey is regarded as the most prestigious customer satisfaction survey in the transportation industry. This is the second year Saia earned this prestigious award, garnering a Quest for Quality accolade in 2003.

      Saia was awarded first place for"Value"in the National LTL Carriers segment along with foremost regional and interregional carriers who surpassed average scores in fundamental areas. In order to be awarded Quest for Quality honors, a carrier must rank above average in each of the following categories: On-Time Performance, Value, Information Technology, Customer Service and Equipment & Operations.

      "We are honored to be recognized by our industry as among the best of the best,"said Saia President and CEO Rick O'Dell."This award is confirmation that the entire Saia team is committed to providing best-in-class service to our customers, as defined by our Customer Service Indicator program."

      The 2006 Quest for Quality awards will be presented to winners at an awards dinner during the Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP) Conference on October 18 in San Antonio.

      Saia Garners Borders Group's Transportation Provider of the Year Award

      DULUTH, GA - June 28, 2006 - Leading Less-Than-Truckload (LTL) carrier Saia has been presented with the 2005 Transportation Provider of the Year Award by Borders Group, Inc., a leading global retailer of books, music and movies with more than 1,200 Borders and Waldenbooks stores worldwide.

      The award is based on criteria measured by Borders Group representatives and applied to all of the company's transportation providers with the goal of continuous improvement in performance and efficiency in supply chain. Specifically, it recognizes carrier performance and delivery of superior client service and value. Selections are made based on a quantitative scoring system that includes on-time service, claims performance, customer service, technology, economic value and innovation.

      "Saia has been an integral solutions and service provider that has contributed significantly to the success of our company's supply chain initiatives. We have an extremely competitive group of vendors and this award is a testament to Saia's dedication and attention to the criteria we have set for outstanding performance,"said Fred Boehler, director of supply chain for Borders Group.

      "We work very closely with Saia's operations, executive, and administrative teams to identify areas of opportunity to obtain the highest level of overall supply chain efficiency,"added Borders Group's Senior Manager of Transportation Tom Allen.

      "Borders Group's supply chain professionals have developed one of the most comprehensive performance scorecards in the industry,"said Saia President and CEO Rick O'Dell."With the high standards and intense competition among a very strong group of transportation providers, our award as Transportation Provider of the Year, is particularly meaningful."

      O'Dell continued"It is great to share this recognition with so many Saia employees that provide outstanding service to Borders Group each week, all year long."
